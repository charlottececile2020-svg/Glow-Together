Women in Computer Science 📖

Explore stories and achievements of women who changed technology and paved the way for the next generation of engineers and creators.

The Legacy of Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace is widely regarded as the first computer programmer. Her notes on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine contained what is now recognized as the first algorithm designed for machine execution. Read more

Grace Hopper and the First Compiler

Rear Admiral Grace Hopper invented the first compiler and helped create COBOL, making programming more accessible and influencing modern software development. Read more

Mary Jackson and NASA Engineering

Mary Jackson became NASA's first Black female engineer, breaking racial and gender barriers while contributing to spaceflight research and inspiring scientists worldwide. Read more

Katherine Johnson's Spaceflight Mathematics

Katherine Johnson’s trajectory calculations were critical to the success of early NASA missions, including John Glenn's orbital flight and the Apollo moon landing program. Read more

Girls Who Code ❤️

Girls Who Code is a nonprofit organization dedicated to closing the gender gap in technology. It teaches computing skills, builds confidence, and creates community for girls and young people who identify as girls so they can become future leaders in computer science.

What Girls Who Code Does

Girls Who Code provides free programs that help young people learn coding, design projects, and discover tech careers. The organization focuses on both skill-building and a supportive, inclusive community.

Key Initiative: Girls Who Code Clubs

The Girls Who Code Clubs initiative offers after-school and summer club experiences where participants work on real projects, learn programming languages, and support each other through teamwork and mentorship.

How You Can Support

Donate, volunteer, or share the mission. Supporting Girls Who Code helps grow the next generation of women in technology and makes tech education more accessible for everyone.

Support Girls Who Code

If you prefer to support local initiatives, consider volunteering time or donating to community shelters, legal aid clinics, and scholarship funds in your region.
